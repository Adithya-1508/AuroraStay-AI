# User Personas

This document maps the primary users of the HospitalityAI platform, documenting their goals, responsibilities, and operational pain points.

---

## 1. Guest (Self-Service User)

### Profile
Guests are travellers staying at the hotel (for business or leisure) who expect high-quality service, immediate responses, and seamless self-service options.

- **Key Goals**:
  - Book rooms, spa slots, or dining tables in natural language.
  - Ask immediate questions about hotel policies, amenities, and local recommendations.
  - Modify or cancel reservations on demand.
- **Pain Points**:
  - Waiting in telephone queues or front desk lines to ask simple questions.
  - Delayed confirmation emails or complex web booking portals.
  - Lack of personalized recommendations for local attractions or services.

---

## 2. Front Desk Staff (Receptionist / Host)

### Profile
The primary operational user responsible for checking in guests, managing reservations, handling physical complaints, and coordinating guest services.

- **Key Goals**:
  - Access a clear, unified view of room availability and check-in rosters.
  - Rapidly resolve reservation issues without navigating multiple archaic systems.
  - Hand off routine queries to automated tools so they can focus on high-touch guest interactions.
- **Pain Points**:
  - High cognitive load from managing incoming calls while handling face-to-face check-ins.
  - Context switching between room reservation systems, spa scheduling software, and messaging apps.

---

## 3. Revenue Manager

### Profile
A business analyst responsible for adjusting pricing tiers, monitoring occupancy pace, and maximizing RevPAR (Revenue Per Available Room).

- **Key Goals**:
  - Accurately forecast room occupancy up to 30 days in advance.
  - Optimize room rates based on predicted cancellation rates and demand spikes.
  - Generate daily and weekly revenue summaries automatically.
- **Pain Points**:
  - Compiling spreadsheet reports manually from different logging databases, leading to delayed pricing strategies.
  - Inability to forecast customer cancellations or predict booking demand shifts early.

---

## 4. Operations Manager

### Profile
The administrator overseeing day-to-day operations, facility maintenance, and housekeeping crews.

- **Key Goals**:
  - Track housekeeping cleaning speeds and room availability in real-time.
  - Automate cleaning task allocations based on room checkout events.
  - Track guest review sentiment across platforms to identify operational bottlenecks.
- **Pain Points**:
  - Lack of visibility into housekeeping completion schedules, resulting in check-in delays.
  - Disconnected workflows (e.g. housekeepers reporting clean rooms on paper, requiring manual front-desk entry).

---

## 5. Administrator

### Profile
The IT or system administrator responsible for configuring the platform, onboarding staff, and maintaining operational health.

- **Key Goals**:
  - Manage user access privileges (Role-Based Access Control).
  - Monitor system health, API latencies, and AI token usages.
  - Review security audit logs and environment secrets.
- **Pain Points**:
  - High operational complexity in managing multiple API keys, models, database credentials, and log files.
